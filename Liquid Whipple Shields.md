TLDR: Could Whipple shields be constructed from some stable or laminar-flowing liquid sheath instead of thi

Regular Whipple Shields use thin layers of solid material to fragment high speed incoming projectiles and thus disperse the force of impact over a larger area of the hull proper. Once a shield has been penetrated at some point, that point needs to be replaced. I wonder whether a sheath of some liquid would also work to fragment the incoming projectile and scatter it across a larger surface. That way, you could replace the liquid lost in the collision from a tank, instead of needing to "re-skin" the Whipple shield.
