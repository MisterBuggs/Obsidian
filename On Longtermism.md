#Random_Idea #Philosophy 
The idea of longtermism proposes that our decisions today should primarily be influenced by their long term outcomes. This is based on utilitarianism and the projection that far more people may live in the future than have lived yet. Hence, the argument goes that investing in a better future (even accepting suffering today in extreme examples of longtermism) creates far more overall joy and reduces far more overall suffering, because it affects so many more people. This hinges on the ideas that human joy / human absence of suffering is an absolute and not a fractional quantity, i.e., The total amount of suffering or joy is the product of the ```(Avg(individual joy) - Avg(individual suffering) x number of people``` and not just the Avg(individual suff)
