#Peer_Review 2026-04-20
Reviewer Summary:

Recommendation:
Major Comments:
1. Quantification and statistical analysis: What kind of outlier analysis was used and using which parameters? If outliers were removed, I highly recommend showing un-altered figures comprising all data points in the supplement, with outliers marked. Figure legends should also contain information on how many / what fraction of data points were removed as outliers. 
2. 
Minor Comments:
3. In the absence of transplantation experiments, please use the term "central trained immunity" with care. 
4. "Perinatal" may be somewhat misleading, as nicotine was administered throughout pregnancy.
5. Mothers absorbed nicotine enterally in this study, instead of the much more common pulmonary delivery in humans. Please discuss this as a potential limitation, considering systemic uptake, biodistribution, first pass, etc.
6. Please discuss the expanded pool of lineage-negative non-HSPC pool.
7. Page 8, "We collected serum (extracellular fluid) from the liver": Serum and extracellular fluid are not synonymous. Did the authors collect serum or extracellular fluid or a general mixed supernatant after isolating liver cells?
8. Page 13, "may be explained by their sequestration to the lung": You measured cell proportions in the lungs in this manuscript. Did you find increased numbers of NK-cells or is this a hypothesis based on the literature?
Language & Proofing:
9. Continuous line numbers are appreciated  during the review process.
10. Quantification and statistical analysis, line 5: There is a period after the +/- sign. 
11. Page 12, line 4: Spelling. 
12. Page 13, "independent of sex and of body weight": "~~of~~" -> "and".
13. Page 13, "infection and infiltrated into the respiratory": "~~infiltrated~~" -> "infiltrate"?
Further Suggestions & Future Directions:
14. Analyzing elderly mice exposed in utero and analyzing the first & second generation off-spring of mice exposed in utero would be highly interesting to capture the scope and persistence of epigenetic changes introduced by perinatal nicotine exposure. In addition, exposing dams before but not during pregnancy or only after birth but while breast feeding may be interesting. 
15. Page 8, 9: Consider analyzing which cells in the liver were responsible for the altered niche signaling, i.e., which cells produced these cytokines, e.g., by intracellular flow cytometry or, as a proxy, by transcriptomics. 
16. Consider sequencing the bone marrow of in utero exposed off-spring, searching for typical, clonal hematopoiesis-associated mutations. 
17. I strongly recommend discussing the potential of behavioral and other physiological changes of nicotine-exposed dams undergoing withdrawal during their off-spring's first days of life, which may cause unique effects on pups that are independent of nicotine exposure itself.
