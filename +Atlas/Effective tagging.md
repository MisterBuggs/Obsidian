- Effective tagging is trivial for unrelated tags (parallel folders / roots) and for fully nested tags (subfolders). 
- Most online tagging advice is advice on effective folder structures in disguise (PARA, Johnny decimal): It addresses good nesting, not the one thing that sets tags apart: Parallelity as a feature. Here's some half decent advice that goes along my practices: Tag new notes immediately, avoid redundant tags for closely related topics, don't overtag (>~ 10 per note)
- In my naive assessment, there are two critical points: Adding tags and choosing roots / starting points for searching the information space during information retrieval. These are related in that they should facilitate discovery in as few clicks as possible while keeping information processable at a glance, i.e., only ever recommending as many nodes or branches to the viewer as they can intuitively and quickly navigate. The latter may be augmented by stable or meta-stable structures, possibly including anchors.
