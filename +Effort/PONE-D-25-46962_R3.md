#Peer_Review [[2026-04-20]]
Reviewer Summary:
In this revision of their primary research manuscript entitled "Integrated single-cell RNA sequencing and Bulk-RNA technologies reveal the immunological characteristics of lactylation related-genes in glioblastoma", Cheng et al. present a secondary analysis of lactate-related genes in published transcriptomics analyses of glioblastoma. Prior comments have mostly been addressed. I appreciate the authors opting for a more modest, conservative interpretation in their abstract, which better reflects the actual findings.

Minor Comments:
2. Line 320: "suggesting" or "indicating" is likely more appropriate here than "confirming".
3. Line 383: "simulated"
Language & Proofing:
4. The language quality in this manuscript is acceptable but shows room for improvement in clarity and expression.
5. Line 49: This sentence implies that machine learning designed a model, which might be slightly misleading wording. The authors designed a model informed by machine learning. 
6. Lines 50-51: "The high-risk group had a high risk outcome." Pleonasm.
7. Line 51: "Immunological analysis" is suggestive of in vitro or in vivo experiments. However, I believe the authors are referencing in silico analyses of ex vivo data here. Consider slight rewording for accuracy. 
8. Lines 80-81: "for tumor cells but tumor cells need a large amount of nutrients and oxygen for rapidly expanding tumor cells" This sentence may benefit from slight re-wording to avoid repeating "tumor cells" three times.  
9. Line 141 still features "Role" instead of "Ro/e".
10. Line 293: There might be an omission here. What "resolution parameter" was used for this analysis?
