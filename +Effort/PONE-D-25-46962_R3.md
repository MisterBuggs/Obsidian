#Peer_Review [[2026-04-20]]
Reviewer Summary:
In this revision of their primary research manuscript entitled "Integrated single-cell RNA sequencing and Bulk-RNA technologies reveal the immunological characteristics of lactylation related-genes in glioblastoma", Cheng et al. present a secondary analysis of lactate-related genes in published transcriptomics analyses of glioblastoma. Prior comments have mostly been addressed. I appreciate the authors opting for a more modest, conservative interpretation in their abstract, which better reflects the actual findings. 
The rationale for this study is strong, the novelty and expected impact are moderately low, mainly owing to the depth of in silico analyses without adequate confirmation in vitro or in vivo.

Recommendation: Minor Revision.
Major Comments:
1. None.
Minor Comments:
2. Line 320: "suggesting" or "indicating" is likely more appropriate here than "confirming".
3. Line 383: "evaluated in silico" or "evaluated indicators of" is likely more appropriate here than simply "evaluated". 
4. Line 446: Calling a model with an AUC of around 60% "reliable" is a stretch.
5. Please also discuss limitations of your work, such as the risk of overfitting and what steps were taken to address this risk.
6. You mention that CD93 were correlated with HLA molecules but does not affect antigen presentation. This is interesting, as it is unexpected. Consider elaborating.
Language & Proofing:
7. The language quality in this manuscript is acceptable but shows room for improvement in clarity and expression.
8. Line 49: This sentence implies that machine learning designed a model, which might be slightly misleading wording. The authors designed a model informed by machine learning. 
9. Lines 50-51: "The high-risk group had a high risk outcome." Pleonasm.
10. Line 51: "Immunological analysis" is suggestive of in vitro or in vivo experiments. However, I believe the authors are referencing in silico analyses of ex vivo data here. Consider slight rewording for accuracy. 
11. Lines 80-81: "for tumor cells but tumor cells need a large amount of nutrients and oxygen for rapidly expanding tumor cells" This sentence may benefit from slight re-wording to avoid repeating "tumor cells" three times.  
12. Line 141 still features "Role" instead of "Ro/e".
13. Line 293: There might be an omission here. What "resolution parameter" was used for this analysis?
14. Lines 430-431: I assume the authors mean "and confirmed" not "which confirmed".
15. Line 502: "confirmed ~~that~~ high".
16. Lines 505 - 508: Circular reasoning.
Further Suggestions:
17. None.
