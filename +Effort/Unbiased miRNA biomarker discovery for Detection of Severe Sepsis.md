#Peer_Review [[2026-04-28]] #ICMx
Reviewer Summary:

Recommendation:
Major Comments:
1. Which reporting guideline was used to prepare this manuscript. Please choose an appropriate reporting guideline, likely from among the various STROBE guidelines, and report your data according to that guidelines. Reporting according to established guidelines is critical for compatibility with later meta-reviews. 
Minor Comments:
2. Title: The term "severe sepsis" should not be used anymore according to the Sepsis-3 guidelines, as it is not well-defined. 
3. Methods: Limiting study inclusion to "patients with neurological hemorrhage" may be a legitimate departmental constraint. This constraint to a very specific patient population may limit generalizability and should thus be included in the manuscript title, e.g., "in a cohort of 116 ICU patients with neurological hemorrhage" or similar. 
4. Methods: Sampling in relation to fever spikes is a little unconventional but interesting. There is rationale for this methodology and I commend the authors for stating this so clearly in the abstract. How where sepsis cases without fever handled, which are common especially in the elderly population? Please also include documentation how your blood draw related to diagnosis and first antibiotic use, as opposed to fever spike. 
Language & Proofing:
5. This may be a function of the editorial manager, but there are currently two different line numberings displayed in the reviewer copy. One of them doesn't match the actual lines, so comments are made using the coherent line numbers.
