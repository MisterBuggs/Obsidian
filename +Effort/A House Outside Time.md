#TTRPG #Call_of_Cthulhu #Writing #History #Worldbuilding [[2026-04-24]]
Picking a scenario writing project back up that I had on my mind for a while. The player characters, heirs to a beautiful Victorian age mansion willed to them by some distant and somewhat estranged uncle or granny, come to spend the weekend, assess the current state of the premises. Possibly, the will demands they spend a night to claim the inheritance. Other than being abandoned, the house is in surprisingly good shape. Every clock in the house appears somehow smashed or broken in some bizzar way (and there are many obvious and less obvious clocks). During the first night, in the early morning, a storm smashes a big branch right through the elaborate *stained glass window* atop the *inaccessible spire*: The tower clock rings and then cuts out and the players find themselves caught in a time loop, restarting them in their beds each night when the tree smashes the window. 

# GM's truth
The stained glass found in many of the windows (?) and watch glasses of this house is really *Glass of Leng*. The original owner, Jonathan Frederick "Uncle" Faraday was a gifted mythos scientist of the Victorian era and had the house built and equipped it specifically to redirect, refract, reflect the flow of time into its central rotund library, where he planned to spend the rest of eternity immortal, shaving just enough off the flow of time to stop aging.
