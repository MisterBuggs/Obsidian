#BayBE #Science_Computer #Manuscript_Resource #Team #Writing #Random_Idea 

Another completely independent idea, this one more appropriate as a project in one of your coding or engineering classes: BayBE currently supports standardized encoding of chemical substances and their similarity. I wonder if a similar custom encoding function could be added to BayBE that encodes the similarity between standard culture media, like the various types of DMEM, F12, Eagle, etc, and their applicability to various primary cell types and immortal cell lines. At least for the standard media, formulations are often non-proprietary and information what cell types and applications are supported by what standard media can probably be scraped from ATCC, other cell banks, and maybe JOVE and other methods journals. For the BayBE project, see https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlehtml/2025/dd/d5dd00050e .
